# EE4080 Final Year Project Guidelines (2025/26)

This document provides a condensed overview of the EE4080 Final Year Project (FYP) guidelines, with a comprehensive focus on all requirements and integration points related to the **Final Project Report**.

---

## 1. Assessment Overview
The FYP is assessed through three main components:
* [cite_start]**Progress (15%)**: Assessed by the supervisor and assessor based on work in Semester A[cite: 7, 34].
* [cite_start]**Final Report & Demonstration (65%)**: This combined component is the primary assessment of the project's technical output[cite: 7].
* [cite_start]**Oral Presentation (20%)**: A mandatory 20-minute session assessing communication skills[cite: 7, 91, 99].

---

## 2. The Final Project Report (Detailed Requirements)
[cite_start]The report is a formal record of the student's problem-solving ability and technical achievement[cite: 38].

### 2.1 Format and Template
* [cite_start]**Standardization**: To ensure a professional appearance for library archiving, all reports must follow the general format in **Appendix III** and use the provided template[cite: 78, 79].
* [cite_start]**General Guidelines**: The format is identical to the "Guide to Laboratories" for formal reports[cite: 74].
* **Project-Specific Content**:
    * [cite_start]**Hardware-oriented**: Should include extensive explanations of circuit design, diagrams, implementation details, and testing[cite: 76].
    * [cite_start]**Software-oriented**: Should focus on user interface, program structure, debugging, and include a user guide (typically as an appendix)[cite: 76].
* [cite_start]**Content Selection**: Students must consult their supervisor to determine exactly what to include or exclude[cite: 77].

### 2.2 Assessment Outcomes
[cite_start]The report is graded against five general outcomes[cite: 80, 82]:
* [cite_start]**Organization**: Logical structure and flow[cite: 82].
* [cite_start]**Content**: Depth of engineering and theoretical analysis[cite: 82].
* [cite_start]**Relevance**: Appropriate use of appendices and relevance of the engineering work[cite: 82].
* [cite_start]**Writing Style & Grammar**: Proficiency in spelling, grammar, and technical style[cite: 82].
* [cite_start]**Presentation of Material**: Effective use of visual aids such as graphs and diagrams[cite: 82].

### 2.3 Academic Honesty and Turnitin
* [cite_start]**Plagiarism**: Defined as using someone else's words or ideas without admission; it is a serious offense[cite: 87].
* [cite_start]**Mandatory Submission**: All reports must be submitted to the **Turnitin system** for a plagiarism check[cite: 88].
* [cite_start]**Originality Report**: The Turnitin originality report **must be attached** to the project report before final submission[cite: 88].
* [cite_start]**Thresholds**: A similarity index exceeding **24%** requires special scrutiny and comments from staff[cite: 88].
* [cite_start]**Referencing Penalty**: Improper referencing that does not constitute plagiarism still incurs a **sub-grade penalty**[cite: 89].
* [cite_start]**Declaration Form**: Students must sign the **Student Declaration Form (Appendix V)**, confirming academic honesty and granting permission for electronic scanning[cite: 87, 88].

## 3. Report Integration with Other Components
The final report is integrated into several other stages of the FYP process:

* [cite_start]**Project Timeline**: In a standard schedule, "Report writing" is allocated to **months 9 and 10**[cite: 55]. [cite_start]For Co-op students, the thesis/report submission is due in **Week 13**[cite: 125].
* [cite_start]**Project Demonstration**: Diagrams and support materials from the report should be available during the live demonstration to assist with explanations[cite: 65].
* [cite_start]**Oral Presentation**: Students are cautioned **not to copy-paste** content directly from the report into presentation slides without proper formatting[cite: 103].
* [cite_start]**Project Competition**: Selection for the FYP competition involves Programme Leaders vetting the quality of the project reports[cite: 157].
* [cite_start]**Project Detail Form (Appendix VI)**: For Co-op students, the objective and abstract in this form serve as the baseline for the final report's assessment[cite: 118].

---

## 4. Responsibilities and Records
* [cite_start]**Supervisor/Assessor Role**: Both use the **Project Assessment Record (PAR) in Appendix IV** to grade the report[cite: 8, 85]. [cite_start]They may adjust weightings for specific outcomes based on the project's nature[cite: 84].
* [cite_start]**Grading**: An "A" grade is reserved for work of exceptionally high quality, representing a minor breakthrough or product-standard result[cite: 15, 183].
