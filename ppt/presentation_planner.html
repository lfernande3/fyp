Title Slide
  • Project title · your name · supervisor · date

Let it sit. Open with: "In IoT networks, every time a sensor wakes up to send a packet, it costs battery. My project asks: how do we tune sleep behaviour to hit both goals at once?"
02Motivation + BG0:20 – 2:202 min
The Problem & Background
  • Left: Billions of MTDs · must sleep to survive · sleeping = missed windows = delay
  • Core tension: minimise delay ↔ maximise lifetime
  • Right: Slotted Aloha with on-demand sleep · 4 states: ACTIVE → IDLE → SLEEP → WAKEUP
  • 3GPP mapping: MICO ≈ on-demand · T3324 ≈ ts · RA-SDT ≈ tw
  • Based on Wang et al., 2024

03System Model2:20 – 3:201 min
System Model & Key Parameters
  • n nodes (100–10,000) · Poisson arrivals λ · unsaturated: λ < μ
  • Power states: PT > PB > PI > PW > PS · slot = 6 ms (3GPP NR)
  • Metrics: mean delay T̄ · lifetime L̄ · success prob p · service rate μ
  • Swept: q (0.01–0.5) · ts (1–100) · n (10–500) · λ (0.001–0.1)

05O2 — Sweeps5:00 – 7:002 min
Parameter Sweep: q and ts (O2)
  • Sweep q (0.01 → 0.35): higher q → lower delay, faster drain
  • Sweep ts (1 → 100): larger ts → longer lifetime, higher delay
  • Both effects monotonic · confirmed across 20 replications
  • q* ≈ 1/n — optimal transmission probability confirmed
  • Bursty traffic amplifies 95th/99th percentile tail delay sharply
slide07_q_sweep.png + slide07_ts_sweep.png side by side
"Every point on those curves is a design choice — q controls where you sit, ts controls which curve you're on." This motivates the next two slides.
06O2 — Scenarios7:00 – 8:001 min
Prioritisation Scenarios (O2)
  • Low-Latency: ts=1, q=2/n → minimal delay · shorter life
  • Balanced: ts=10, q=1/n → moderate trade-off
  • Battery-Life: ts=50, q=0.5/n → years of life · higher delay
  • Quantified gains/losses vs balanced baseline

07O3 — Pareto8:00 – 10:302 min 30 s⭐ HERO
Optimisation & Pareto Frontier (O3)
  • Grid search over full (q, ts) space → 2-D lifetime/delay heatmaps
  • Pareto frontier: each ts value → one best (max-lifetime, min-delay) point
  • Moving along frontier: increasing ts trades delay for lifetime monotonically
  • Design rule: ts is the primary knob · q fine-tunes within a chosen ts
  • q* = 1/n is near-optimal across the entire frontier

08O3 — Duty Cycle10:30 – 11:301 min
On-Demand Sleep vs Duty-Cycling (O3)
  • Duty-cycling: wakes on fixed periodic schedule regardless of arrivals
  • On-demand: only wakes when a packet actually arrives
  • On-demand saves 20–40% more energy at equivalent delay
  • Dominates duty-cycling across all tested ts and awake fractions
slide09_duty_cycle_comparison.png
"This isn't just a claim from the paper — we simulated both schemes under identical conditions and on-demand wins every time." 60 s, don't linger.
09O4 — Validation11:30 – 13:001 min 30 s
3GPP Validation & Design Guidelines (O4)
  • MICO → on-demand · T3324 → ts · PSM → sleep · RA-SDT → tw
  • 4 standard scenarios: NB-IoT (2s & 60s T3324) + NR mMTC (2-step & 4-step RA-SDT)
  • p, μ, T̄, L̄ within ±5% of analytical formulas
  • Convergence: <5% error at 10⁵ slots · stable by 10⁶ slots
  • Design guideline table: recommended ts & q* for each λ
